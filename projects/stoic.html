BrickMan Superhero Project

Context

The Movie Webpage, primarily called BrickMan, was created with the help of me (Rain) and Jehan (my partner). We used our knowledge of HTML and CSS to create a tragedy-based webpage movie.

Content

There's no good project without a plan so me and my partner created a text file, plan.md, md standing for markdown. There are requirements for the movie webpage like elements and selectors in the webpage we have to agree on, and specialize in it. Beginning with HTML requirements:

  • headers (Jehan)
  • paragraphs (both)
  • images (Rain)
  • imported and Original Google Fonts (Rain)
  • divs and spans (Rain)
  • inputs and submit (Jehan)

CSS requirements:

  • all selectors (Jehan)
  • Box Model- padding, margin, border properties (Rain)
  • images (Rain)
  • Background and color scheme (Jehan)

Time to Reflect

From what I know, me and my partner only faced one challenge on how the story should be formatted. This meant where to place the images and the storyline which we later figured out (straight down, nothing fancy). However, I faced a big obstacle while making the paragraph and the image side by side. I watched YouTube tutorials and tried some random properties, testing out each one of them. Eventually, I gave up because I realized I was wasting time on something we hadn't learned yet. Before the takeaways, Mr. Mueller has amazing advice so credit to him (I mean it).

Takeaways

  • Keep it simple and don't stress about it being perfect (Rephrased version of Mr. Mueller's advice)
  • "Begin with the structure, then the decoration, and top it off with organization"= Work on the storyline then focus on CSS selectors, divs, and spans
  • Taking time to process my ideas and finalizing them
  • "Don't be ahead, be on task"= Use the knowledge I "KNOW" from CSS and HTML to complete my project
  • Similar to the third bullet point, take mental breaks by taking your mind off coding (going outside or gaming). Let your mind clean itself and give it some fun (not too much).

If I had a "Next Step", I would...

I would try to add a third page based on BrickMan's life during his redemption (recommend reading the movie webpage to understand) and a lesson, teaching others to appreciate what is given to them.
